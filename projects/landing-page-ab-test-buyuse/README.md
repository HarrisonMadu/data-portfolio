# 🏠 Buy&Use: E-Commerce Landing Page UX Redesign & A/B Test

**Client:** Buy&Use – Online retailer of household appliances  
**Role:** UX Designer & Growth Analyst  
**Timeline:** June–July 2026  
**Impact:** ↑ 41% conversion rate, ↓ 35% bounce rate

---

## 📋 Executive Summary

**Buy&Use**, an online retailer of household appliances, was struggling with low conversion rates on their flagship product pages. Despite high traffic volume, users were abandoning before completing purchases.

**My Role:** Led UX research, redesign, and A/B testing to improve conversion rates.

### 🏆 Key Results

| Metric | Before | After | Improvement |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Conversion Rate** | 1.7% | 2.4% | ↑ 41% |
| **Bounce Rate** | 72% | 47% | ↓ 35% |
| **Average Order Value** | ₹3,850 | ₹4,620 | ↑ 20% |
| **Add-to-Cart Rate** | 5.2% | 8.9% | ↑ 71% |
| **Revenue per Visitor** | ₹65.45 | ₹110.88 | ↑ 69% |

## 🎯 1. Problem Statement

### The Challenge

Buy&Use had a strong product catalog but poor conversion performance:

- **High traffic (25K/month)** but **low conversions (1.7%)**
- **72% bounce rate** – users weren't engaging with product pages
- **5.2% add-to-cart rate** – well below industry benchmark (15%)
- **Cart abandonment at 68%** – users left during checkout

### User Pain Points (From Research)

| Issue | Impact |
| :--- | :--- |
| **❌ Poor product images** – Low-quality, inconsistent photos | Users couldn't visualize the product in their home |
| **❌ Unclear specifications** – Confusing product descriptions | Users left to compare elsewhere |
| **❌ Missing reviews** – No social proof | Users didn't trust the product quality |
| **❌ Slow mobile load time (5.2s)** | 40% of mobile users abandoned |
| **❌ Hidden shipping costs** | Users frustrated at checkout |

### User Personas

**Primary Persona: Meera (35, Working Mother)**

> *"I don't have time to go to stores. I need quick, reliable buying decisions for my home. I want to see how the product will help me, real reviews, and clear pricing – all in one place."*

- **Needs:** Time-saving, trust in product quality, clear comparisons
- **Pain points:** Poor photos, unclear specs, no reviews
- **Devices:** 70% mobile, 30% desktop

**Secondary Persona: Raj (28, New Homeowner)**

> *"I'm setting up my first home. I need to understand which appliances will last. I research heavily before buying."*

- **Needs:** Detailed specs, warranty info, long-term value
- **Pain points:** Missing technical details, no comparison tools
- **Devices:** 60% desktop, 40% mobile

## 🔍 2. UX Research & Discovery

### Research Methods

| Method | Findings |
| :--- | :--- |
| **🔎 Heuristic Evaluation** | 18 usability violations identified (consistency, feedback, recognition) |
| **📊 Analytics Review** | High exit rates on product pages, especially on mobile |
| **🎥 Session Recordings (50+ sessions)** | Users confused by product details; multiple clicks wasted |
| **📝 User Surveys (N=200)** | 82% said photos were the #1 factor in purchase decisions |
| **🛒 Cart Abandonment Survey** | 65% cited hidden shipping costs as main reason |
| **🌐 Competitive Analysis** | 5 competitors (Amazon, Flipkart, Pepperfry, etc.) – all had 360° product views, reviews, clear pricing |

### 💡 Key Insight

> *"Users buy confidence, not products. Buy&Use wasn't building enough trust at the product page level."*

---

## 🎨 3. UX Design Process

### Design Principles Applied

1. **Trust-First Design** – Build confidence through social proof and transparent info
2. **Mobile-First** – 70% of users are on mobile
3. **Progressive Disclosure** – Show essential info first, details on request
4. **Visual Hierarchy** – Guide users to conversion through clear CTAs

### Before & After Comparison

| Element | Before | After |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Hero Section** | Generic stock image + product name | ✅ Lifestyle image + benefit headline + price + rating |
| **Product Images** | 2 low-res images | ✅ 360° view, zoom, 6 high-res images |
| **Product Description** | Long, text-heavy paragraph | ✅ Bullet points + icons (benefits) |
| **Reviews** | None visible above the fold | ✅ Carousel of 5-star reviews + ratings |
| **Shipping Info** | Only visible at checkout | ✅ Free shipping badge + delivery estimate |
| **CTA Button** | Small, below the fold | ✅ Sticky "Add to Cart" + "Buy Now" buttons |
| **Trust Badges** | None | ✅ Payment security, warranty, 30-day returns |
| **Compare Tool** | None | ✅ Compare with similar products |

## 📊 4. A/B Test Design

### Hypothesis

*"By redesigning the product page to feature high-quality lifestyle images, prominent reviews, clear pricing, and a sticky CTA, we will increase conversion rate by at least 25%."*

### Test Structure

| Metric | Control (Original) | Variant (Redesigned) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Sample Size** | 10,000 visitors | 10,000 visitors |
| **Traffic Split** | 50% | 50% |
| **Duration** | 21 days | 21 days |
| **Statistical Confidence** | 95% | — |

### Key Metrics Tracked

1. **Primary:** Conversion rate (purchase completion)
2. **Secondary:** Add-to-cart rate, bounce rate, time on page
3. **Business:** Revenue per visitor, average order value
4. **User Experience:** Scroll depth, heatmap engagement

## 🔑 7. Key Learnings

### ✅ What Worked

1. **Lifestyle images > Product shots** – Users connected emotionally with the product
2. **Reviews matter most** – 200+ reviews generated higher trust
3. **Mobile-first design** – 70% of users, 58% conversion lift
4. **Sticky CTA drives action** – Always visible, always ready
5. **Free shipping badge** – Reduced cart abandonment
6. **Trust badges** – 30% increase in user confidence

### 💡 What I'd Do Differently

1. **Test video content** – Product demos could increase conversion further
2. **Add cross-sell suggestions** – "People also bought" to increase AOV
3. **A/B test pricing display** – Test different discount formats
4. **Implement user-generated content** – Customer photos with products
